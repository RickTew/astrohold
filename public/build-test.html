VARIANT 1 — COMPASS CLUSTER SANDBOX

Dial in the center. Four toggle pips orbit it at compass points (N / E / S / W). Compact — fits in roughly the size of a single HUD tile. Best when there's limited horizontal real estate.

VARIANT 2 — BRIDGE PANEL SANDBOX

Chamfered horizontal panel. Dial on the left, 2×2 grid of toggle buttons on the right. Reads as a "main console" you'd see on a bridge. Most room for additional toggles if we expand later.

VARIANT 3 — COCKPIT STACK SANDBOX

Vertical chamfered panel. Dial sits on top; a horizontal strip of toggles runs underneath. Cockpit-instrument pattern (gauge over switch panel). Taller than wide — fits a corner of the HUD.

ALL-IN-ONE-CIRCLE VARIANTS EVERYTHING INSIDE THE DIAL

Three takes on the "one circle does it all" idea. Outer ring = speed (arc + colored ticks). Inside the dial face is where the toggles live. V4 = compact 2×2 grid in the middle. V5 = single big center button (most minimal — less is more taken literally). V6 = concentric inner ring of pips at 12/3/6/9.

VARIANT 5 — MINIMAL CORE (SINGLE CENTER BUTTON) SANDBOX

Outer ring sets speed. ONE big button in the middle — the "everything-off" mute (silences music + SFX + speech in one tap). Tap-and-hold (in production) could reveal a sub-menu with individual toggles. Most minimal of all six variants.

VARIANT 6 — INNER PIP RING (4 TOGGLES AT 12/3/6/9) SANDBOX

Speed pointer + arc on the outer ring (no inner ticks — toggles replace them visually). 4 toggle pips arranged on a smaller inner ring at clock positions. Heaviest cockpit-instrument feel.
